A Brief, Baffling History

Discovered in 1899 in a forgotten wing of the Bodleian Library, Professor Alistair Von Hipporoo was found lecturing to a parliament of owls. His historical record is a tapestry of the absurd, with speeches delivered from the pouches of kangaroos and philosophical debates held in hippo-filled rivers. He is the only known intellectual to have successfully argued that the meaning of life is "a comfortable wallow and a mighty leap."
